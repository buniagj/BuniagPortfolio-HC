MY EXPERTISE

English Teaching

I taught English to Junior High School students for seven academic years. As a teacher, I never focus on the accuracy alone; I target fluency, too. Hence, make mistakes as many as you can because only by then we learn real time. Establishing real-life connections is also a must, so the students wouldn't need to wonder why they have to learn English at their age. Teaching never stops in the classroom. When students want to learn more, I give my time to teach them reading and speaking. Let's learn together!

Creative Writing

Poetry has been the most effective catharsis I have ever tried in my life (no offense to the mental health specialist that I consulted in 2022). It never judges the vulgarity and depths of my imagination, and the motives for my "lingering in the deepest darkest corners of my sanity". Most people no longer utilize words into poems for better presentation. No more Romeos to the Juliets, but let's not lose hope for this endeavor must never die as long as the New Romantics still know how to utter the tactics of the old. I'll be with you.

Voice Narration

A few years back, I noticed how my voice changed drastically: from a well-modulated to a high-pitched one. As some sort of memento, I recorded my voice for the first time as I was reading my favorite book "The Little Prince" by Antoine de Saint-ExupÃ©ry in 2019. Since then, I have recorded 7 audiobooks, and 6 of my own work from my collection "A Profound Catharsis". Impliedly, I want my young voice to be heard by my future children and grand children... a voice frozen in time.

Web Development

I thought I wanted to be this tiny spark of light that guides my students in a seemingly dark endless tunnel. Unbeknownst to me, seven long years consumed the dying minute spark I have. Now, I have none left. I think it's about time that I give someone's light a chance to lead me on a different path: Web Development. Thanks to Kodego, little by little I am rekindling my own spark. Step by step, project after project, I am moving towards the end of another seemingly dark endless tunnel.
